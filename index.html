Biography

Xidong Mu is a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) with the Centre for Wireless Innovation (CWI), Queen's University Belfast, U.K., since August 2024. He currently leads the Smart Wireless Innovation and Future Technologies (SWIFT) Lab. He was a Postdoctoral Researcher with the STAR Lab at Queen Mary University of London (July 2022–July 2024). He received the Ph.D. degree in Information and Communication Engineering from the Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Beijing, China, in June 2022.

Dr. Mu serves as the Public Engagement and Social Networks Coordinator of IEEE ComSoc SPCC-TC and the Secretary of the IEEE ComSoc Next Generation Multiple Access (NGMA) Emerging Technology Initiative. He is also an Editor of IEEE Transactions on Communications. Dr. Mu is the recipient of the [2023 IEEE ComSoc Outstanding Young Researcher Award for the Europe, Middle East and Africa Region] and the co-recipient of the [2024 IEEE Communications Society Heinrich Hertz Award for Best Communications Letter], the [2022 IEEE ComSoc SPCC TC Best Paper Award], the [ISWCS 2022 Best Paper Award], and the [IEEE VTC2022-Fall Best Student Paper Award].

Prospective PhD Students (Fall 2026)

I'm always looking for self-motivated students who want to pursue a Ph.D. degree on 6G research directions in SWIFT Lab @ QUB, including (but not limited to):

  • Pinching-Antenna Systems (PASS)
  • Simultaneously Transmitting and Reflecting Surfaces (STARS)
  • Near-field communications and sensing
  • Next Generation Multiple Access (NGMA)

Students with strong backgrounds in wireless communications, signal processing, mathematical optimization, and machine learning are encouraged to apply. If you are interested in joining us, please email your CV, transcripts, and ranking (if available).

I also welcome applications for post-doctoral researchers and visiting students/scholars. I’m happy to support proposal applications and host your visit at CWI, QUB.
